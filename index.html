Understanding Your Skin

✦ What You're Dealing With

Sensitized Skin

Sensitized skin is not a skin type — it's a temporary skin condition that can happen to anyone. It develops when the skin barrier becomes compromised by external factors, allowing irritants to penetrate more easily and triggering a reactive, inflamed response.

The key difference: sensitive skin is something you're born with. Sensitized skin is something that happened to yours — and with the right routine, it can fully heal.

🪞

Your Symptoms

  • Redness — diffuse flushing or blotchy patches, often worse after cleansing or temperature changes
  • Tightness — a pulling, stretched feeling after washing, signalling lipid depletion and low moisture
  • No stinging — a positive sign; your barrier is impaired but hasn't reached severe reactivity
  • Possible dullness or uneven tone from chronic low-level inflammation
  • Skin may feel rough or slightly dehydrated to the touch
🔍

Common Causes

  • Over-exfoliation — too many acids, scrubs, or retinol stripping the barrier faster than it can rebuild
  • Harsh cleansers — sulphate-heavy or high-pH cleansers disrupting the acid mantle
  • Environmental stress — UV, pollution, heat, and humidity changes that degrade barrier lipids
  • Fragrance & alcohol in products causing repeated micro-irritation over time
  • Stress & poor sleep — cortisol spikes reduce ceramide production
🧬

What's Happening Inside

  • Impaired lipid barrier — reduced ceramides, cholesterol & fatty acids create microscopic gaps
  • Elevated TEWL — transepidermal water loss increases, causing persistent dehydration and tightness
  • Mast cell activation — immune cells release histamine, triggering redness and reactive flushes
  • Weakened acid mantle — pH rises above 5.5, disrupting the skin microbiome and enzyme activity
  • Nerve endings become more exposed, lowering the skin's irritation threshold
💡

Sensitized vs. Sensitive

  • Sensitive skin is a genetic skin type — permanent, present from birth, cannot be cured
  • Sensitized skin is acquired — triggered by lifestyle, products, or environment
  • Sensitized skin can fully recover with a consistent barrier-focused routine
  • Both types benefit from gentle, fragrance-free, ceramide-rich products
  • Your skin shows mild-to-moderate sensitization — redness & tightness without stinging is an early-stage sign, very recoverable

⚠ Triggers to Avoid During Recovery

Chemical Exfoliants (AHA/BHA) Retinol / Retinoids Fragrance & Parfum Denatured Alcohol Essential Oils Hot Water Physical Scrubs High-pH Cleansers Vitamin C (L-Ascorbic Acid) Benzoyl Peroxide

Skin Recovery Phases

🌱 Phase 1 — Stabilise (Week 1–2)

The priority is stopping further damage and reducing inflammation. Less is more. Stick to only the core calming products and avoid all active ingredients (acids, retinol, vitamin C) until redness and tightness subside.

  • Cleanse once at night only if skin is very reactive (just rinse with cool water in AM)
  • Focus on: Cleanser → Centella Ampoule → Ceramide Moisturizer → SPF (AM only)
  • Avoid: fragrance, alcohol, exfoliants, hot water, over-touching face
  • Reassess after 7 days — if redness is reducing, move to Phase 2

💧 Phase 2 — Hydrate & Repair (Week 3–5)

Skin is calmer. Now layer in hydration and begin consistent barrier repair. Introduce the full routine gradually — add one new product every 3–4 days to monitor any reaction.

  • Full morning routine: Cleanser → Toner → Ampoule → Moisturizer → SPF
  • Full evening routine: Double Cleanse → Toner → Ampoule → Brightening Cream → Ceramide Moisturizer
  • Consistency matters more than intensity — use each product daily
  • Patch test any new products on inner arm for 48 hours before face
  • Goal: Skin feels plump and tightness is noticeably reduced

✨ Phase 3 — Strengthen & Glow (Week 6–10)

Barrier is rebuilding. Redness is largely managed. You can maintain the full routine and begin noticing the brightening and tone-evening benefits of niacinamide and centella actives.

  • Continue full morning and evening routines consistently
  • Optionally introduce a low-concentration niacinamide or gentle enzyme exfoliant 1–2× per week (not an acid)
  • Monitor: If any redness flares, scale back immediately to Phase 2
  • Lifestyle support: 7–9 hours sleep, reduce sugar/alcohol, stay hydrated
  • SPF every. single. day — UV is the #1 barrier aggressor

👑 Phase 4 — Maintain & Radiate (Month 3+)

Your barrier is healthy, redness is minimal, and skin feels resilient. This is the long-term maintenance phase — the goal is to keep the skin in this happy place.

  • Continue your full routine — it's working, don't abandon what's not broken
  • Rotate moisturizers based on season: gel in humid weather, ceramide cream in dry/cool season
  • Can now slowly introduce brightening actives (Vitamin C serum at 5–10%) on alternate evenings
  • Schedule a skin health check-in every 4 weeks: How does skin feel? Adjust accordingly
  • Celebrate your progress — sensitized skin healing takes patience and consistency 🌸

Makeup Application Order

💡 Before You Begin — Skincare First

Always complete your full morning skincare routine and allow at least 5–10 minutes for all layers to fully absorb before applying any makeup. Applying makeup onto tacky or unabsorbed skincare causes pilling, patchy coverage, and can irritate sensitized skin. If in a rush, a quick 60-second press-and-hold with clean palms speeds up absorption.

1
Spot Conceal & Prime — ONLYOU BB Cream

After skincare has fully absorbed, apply the ONLYOU Concealer Brightening & Isolating BB Cream first. Use a fingertip to tap a small amount directly onto any redness spots, dark circles, or blemish marks and gently pat to blend. Then take a slightly larger amount and press all over the face for an even, brightened base.

Why first? The isolating primer function in this product creates a protective barrier between your skincare and subsequent makeup layers — it helps everything sit better, last longer, and prevents makeup from sinking into pores. Applying it before foundation is the correct layering order for 3-in-1 BB creams.

Minimal day option: Worn alone over SPF, the ONLYOU BB provides a polished, naturally brightened finish without any additional base needed.

2
Choose Your Base — Skin Tint (Light Day) or Cushion Foundation (Full Day)

Option A — No-Makeup Day: Apply ESQA Minimalist Blurring Serum Skin Tint in Vanilla with fingertips or a damp beauty sponge. Warm a pea-sized amount between fingers and press outward from center. Build a second layer only where coverage is needed (redness spots, around nose).

Option B — Polished Day: Apply SKINTIFIC Cover Glow Cushion using the included puff with pressing motions (never dragging). For maximum glow, press 2–3 times on areas you want to look dewy. Or swap for TIRTIR Mask Fit Red Cushion for fuller, longer-wearing coverage.

Mixing tip: On days you want medium coverage with a skin-tint freshness, apply the ESQA Skin Tint first, then lightly tap one layer of either cushion only on redness-prone areas (nose, cheeks).

3
Set with Translucent Loose Powder — Second Date

Using a large, fluffy powder brush, pick up a small amount of Second Date Translucent Loose Powder and tap off excess. Lightly dust over T-zone and any areas prone to creasing or shine. For the rest of the face, either skip or do one whisper-light pass to set without over-matting.

For sensitized/dry skin: Avoid heavy powder application — focus only on the nose and forehead. Heavy powder on dry skin emphasises texture. The translucent shade ensures no colour cast or flashback from the TIRTIR or SKINTIFIC SPF underneath.

4
Finish & Lock

Optionally mist face with a gentle hydrating mist (centella water works beautifully) to meld all layers together and give a natural, skin-like finish. This "baking-into-skin" technique blends the powder seamlessly so nothing looks dry or settled.

Let everything set for 2 minutes before going out — the layered SPF from skincare + cushion foundation gives excellent UV coverage throughout the day.

🪷 Makeup Tips for Sensitized Skin

  • Always check that makeup tools (sponges, brushes) are clean — dirty tools carry bacteria that trigger flare-ups
  • When skin is in a reactive phase, opt for the ESQA Skin Tint only — it's the gentlest option with minimal formula on skin
  • Remove makeup as early as possible in the evening — the longer it sits, the more it can stress sensitized skin
  • Use the Pond's Biome or Somethinc cleanser for makeup removal — always double cleanse if wearing cushion foundation + loose powder
  • Cushion SPF layers (SKINTIFIC SPF50+, TIRTIR SPF40) count toward your daily sun protection but always ensure skincare SPF is the true base
  • Avoid applying makeup over visibly irritated, broken, or inflamed skin — let skin breathe until the flare calms down
  • Store cushion foundations away from heat and direct sunlight to preserve their formula integrity
