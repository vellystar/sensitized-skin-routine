🌿 Sensitized Skin

Understanding your skin type is the foundation of every great routine.

⚠️ Sensitized vs. Sensitive Skin — Key Difference

Sensitized skin is not a skin type you're born with — it develops from external factors like over-exfoliation, pollution, stress, harsh products, or UV damage. Unlike naturally sensitive skin, it can be repaired. Your skin barrier is compromised, causing redness, stinging, tightness, and reactivity.

🔴

Common Symptoms

Redness, stinging or burning when applying products, tightness after cleansing, itching, flaking, blotchy appearance, and breakouts from products that didn't used to cause issues.

🔬

Root Cause

A damaged skin barrier (stratum corneum). The lipid matrix between skin cells is disrupted, allowing irritants to penetrate while moisture escapes — called Trans-Epidermal Water Loss (TEWL).

🚫

Avoid These

High-percentage acids (AHA/BHA), alcohol-based toners, physical scrubs, fragrance-heavy products, essential oils, retinol until repaired, SLS cleansers, and very hot water.

💚

Skin-Friendly Ingredients

Centella Asiatica (Cica), Ceramides, Hyaluronic Acid, Snail Mucin, Niacinamide (low %), Panthenol, Allantoin, Madecassoside, Beta-Glucan, and Zinc.

💧

Barrier Repair Goals

Restore skin's natural moisture factor (NMF), rebuild the lipid layer with ceramides, reduce inflammation, and strengthen the acid mantle with low pH cleansers.

⏱

Recovery Timeline

With a consistent barrier-supportive routine, improvement begins in 2–4 weeks. Full barrier repair takes 1–3 months. Patience and consistency are key.

🧴 Golden Rules for Sensitized Skin

  • Introduce one new product at a time — wait 1–2 weeks before adding another.
  • Always patch test on your inner arm or behind your ear for 24–48 hours.
  • Less is more: simplify during flare-ups to just cleanser + moisturizer + SPF.
  • Never skip sunscreen — UV damage is the #1 barrier destroyer.
  • Use lukewarm water only — hot water strips the lipid barrier.
  • Pat dry with a clean soft cloth, never rub.
  • Apply products to damp skin for better absorption.

🔄 Skin Recovery Phases

Your sensitized skin heals in stages — understand where you are and what your routine should focus on.

🗺️ How to Use These Phases

Think of your recovery as a journey: repair → stability → enhancement. Each phase has a different goal, product emphasis, and things to avoid. Move to the next phase only when skin feels comfortable for 1–2 consecutive weeks. If a flare-up happens, step back to Phase 1 temporarily — that's completely normal.

🧪 Routine Step Phases

Every step in your routine belongs to a layering phase. These badges appear on each routine step to explain why that step happens when it does.

Cleanse Phase

Remove & Reset

Removes makeup, SPF, sebum, and pollutants. Resets skin pH and prepares a clean canvas. Uses low-pH formulas to protect the acid mantle.

Prep Phase

Balance & Hydrate

Toner restores moisture balance post-cleanse and primes skin for better absorption of the active layers that follow.

Treat Phase

Target & Repair

Serums and ampoules deliver concentrated actives (Cica, HA, Niacinamide) on damp skin for maximum penetration. Thinnest-to-thickest rule applies.

Seal Phase

Lock & Nourish

Moisturizers seal in all previous layers and deliver lipids and ceramides. Creates the barrier film that prevents TEWL.

Protect Phase

Shield (AM Only)

Physical SPF is the absolute final skincare step every morning. Must be applied last and allowed to form before any makeup.

Base Phase

Even & Cover

Foundation, skin tint, and cushion unify skin tone. Applied after SPF with press-and-roll to avoid disrupting the barrier beneath.

Finish Phase

Set & Perfect

Concealer corrects and powder sets the base for wear. Final step that polishes the look without overcaking.

Repair Phase

Restore (PM Only)

Evening's deep ceramide and Cica layers work overnight during skin's peak cell renewal window (11pm–2am). The most intensive healing happens while you sleep.

🌤 Mid-Day Touch Up

Refresh SPF and makeup without starting over — smart reapplication for all-day protection.

⏰ When to Reapply

SPF must be reapplied every 2 hours outdoors, every 4 hours indoors near windows, and after sweating or towel contact. This touch-up ritual applies to all three recovery phases — even Phase 1 skin needs mid-day SPF reapplication.

🌬️

Step 1 — SPF Reapply (Protect Phase)

Use Bening's Sunscreen Spray. Hold 15–20cm from face, close eyes, spray evenly over face, hairline, and neck. Let mist settle (10 sec), then gently pat with ring fingertip or clean beauty blender to press into skin without disturbing makeup. Most important mid-day step — every phase.

🫧

Step 2 — Blot First

Before makeup touch-up, press blotting paper on oily zones — lift straight off, don't rub. Removes excess sebum without disrupting SPF or makeup base. Blotting first prevents pressing new product into oil buildup, which causes caking.

✨

Step 3 — Cushion Refresh (Base Phase · Phase 2–3)

After spray SPF settles, press TIRTIR or Skintific cushion puff lightly on faded areas (under-eyes, nose, chin). One gentle press per zone. This also adds a micro layer of SPF from the cushion's own formula.

🌫️

Step 4 — Set & Finish (Finish Phase · Phase 2–3)

After cushion touch-up: spray Bening's SPF spray first, pat in gently. Then mist with PIXY Aqua-Beauty Matte Mist to re-mattify and refresh the whole face. Skip the LOREAL mist for mid-day touch-ups — it's a setting spray best used on fresh makeup, not over worn layers.

💧

Hydration Top-Up (Seal Phase)

If skin feels tight mid-day, press a small amount of Somethinc Ceramic Skin Gel onto dry cheek areas before cushion touch-up. Absorbs quickly without interfering with SPF spray. A facial mist can also be pressed in before reapplication.
